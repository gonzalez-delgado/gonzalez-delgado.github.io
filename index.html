Hi!

I am currently a PhD student in the Statistics and Optimization Team at the Toulouse Institute of Mathematics (IMT) and in the Robotics and Interactions Team at LAAS-CNRS. I work under the supervision of Pierre Neuvial (IMT) and Juan Cortés (LAAS). I am also a member of the Mathematics, Biology & Health group at IMT and of the Apprentissage, Optimisation, Complexité (AOC) project-team. My doctoral research is funded by the Labex CIMI.
Here is my CV as of 2023-10.

Research

My current research work aims at defining statistical tools for the structural analysis of globular and Intrinsically Disordered Proteins (IDP). For this purpose, we make use of Optimal Transport Theory to define goodness-of-fit tests on the protein conformational space, and assess the suitability of Wasserstein distance to describe and compare protein ensembles, which can be described by measures on non-euclidean spaces as the two-dimensional flat torus or the special euclidean space of three dimensions. We also explore the definition of clustering methods in the conformational space, together with a post-clustering inference analysis.

Keywords: Optimal Transport, Wasserstein distance, Goodness-of-fit test, Post-clustering inference, Structural Biology, Protein structural analysis, Intrinsically Disordered Proteins (IDP).
